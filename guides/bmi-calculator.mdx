The BMI (Body Mass Index) calculator on the FitStart Planner homepage gives you a quick, standardized snapshot of where your weight sits relative to your height. It takes two numbers — your height in centimetres and your weight in kilograms — and returns a single value alongside a color-coded category and a plain-language health message. It's one of the fastest ways to establish a baseline before you start a new fitness routine.

## Opening the Calculator

Navigate to the FitStart Planner homepage (the FitStart Planner homepage). Scroll down past the introduction section until you reach the **Tools** area — the BMI Calculator is the first tool on the page.

## Calculating Your BMI

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your height">
    Type your height in centimetres into the **Height (cm)** field. For example, enter `170` for 170 cm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your weight">
    Type your weight in kilograms into the **Weight (kg)** field. For example, enter `70` for 70 kg.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the form">
    Click the **Calculate BMI** button. Your result appears immediately below the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read your result">
    The result panel shows three pieces of information: your **BMI value**, your **category label**, and a short **health message** tailored to your category.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How BMI Is Calculated

FitStart Planner uses the standard BMI formula:

> **BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)²**

Your height in centimetres is converted to metres automatically before the calculation runs.

**Worked example:** For a person who is **170 cm tall** and weighs **70 kg**:

> 70 ÷ (1.70)² = 70 ÷ 2.89 ≈ **24.2 → Normal Weight**

## BMI Categories

The result is color-coded so you can understand your category at a glance.

| Category | BMI Range | Indicator color |
|---|---|---|
| Underweight | Below 18.5 | Blue |
| Normal Weight | 18.5 – 24.9 | Green |
| Overweight | 25.0 – 29.9 | Orange |
| Obese | 30.0 and above | Red |

Each category also displays a short health message — for example, a Normal Weight result encourages you to maintain your current habits, while other categories suggest speaking with a healthcare professional.

<Warning>
  BMI is a population-level screening tool, not a clinical diagnosis. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or fat distribution — all of which affect health outcomes. Do not use your BMI result to make medical decisions. Consult a qualified healthcare provider for personalised medical advice.
</Warning>

<Info>
  Athletes and people with high muscle mass often have a BMI in the Overweight range despite being in excellent health, because muscle is denser than fat. Conversely, a Normal Weight BMI does not guarantee good metabolic health. Use the result as a starting point for a broader conversation, not a final verdict.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Recalculate your BMI every four to six weeks as you progress through your FitStart routine. A gradual shift in your BMI value — even a small one — can be a motivating indicator that your consistency is paying off.
</Tip>
