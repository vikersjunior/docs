Good nutrition is the other half of any successful fitness plan. The [Nutrition page](/features/nutrition) in FitStart Planner gives you three practical tools to build healthier eating habits without overwhelming you with complex rules: a simple visual guide to building balanced meals, a personalised hydration calculator, and an instant meal idea generator when you're not sure what to cook.

## The Balanced Plate

The balanced plate method is one of the most beginner-friendly frameworks for eating well — no calorie counting or macro tracking required. Visualise your plate divided into four sections and fill each one as follows:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="½ Plate — Vegetables & Fruit" icon="apple-whole">
    Fill half your plate with colourful vegetables and fruit. These provide fibre, vitamins, and minerals that support energy, recovery, and overall health.
  </Card>
  <Card title="¼ Plate — Lean Protein" icon="drumstick-bite">
    Dedicate a quarter of your plate to lean protein. Protein repairs muscle tissue after workouts and keeps you feeling full between meals.
  </Card>
  <Card title="¼ Plate — Quality Carbohydrates" icon="wheat-awn">
    Fill the remaining quarter with quality carbohydrates such as whole grains or starchy vegetables. Carbs are your body's primary fuel source during exercise.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Small Portion — Healthy Fats" icon="droplet">
    Add a small amount of healthy fat — such as olive oil, avocado, or nuts — to support hormone function, joint health, and the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  You don't need to measure portions precisely. Use your plate as a visual guide at every meal and adjust over time based on how your energy and hunger levels feel.
</Tip>

## Hydration Calculator

Staying hydrated directly affects your workout performance and recovery. The hydration calculator on the Nutrition page gives you a personalised daily water target based on your body weight and how active you are.

### How to Use It

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your body weight">
    Type your weight in **kilograms** into the body weight field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your activity level">
    Choose the option that best matches your typical week:
    - **Light** — mostly sedentary, light activity 1–2 days per week
    - **Moderate** — active 3–5 days per week
    - **High** — active most days or doing intense workouts
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read your target">
    Click **Calculate** and your recommended daily water intake appears in **litres**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### The Formula

The calculator uses the following formula to produce your result:

```
Daily Water (L) = Body Weight (kg) × 0.033 × Activity Multiplier
```

| Activity Level | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Light | 1.0 |
| Moderate | 1.15 |
| High | 1.3 |

**Example:** A person weighing 70 kg with a Moderate activity level would get:
`70 × 0.033 × 1.15 = 2.66 litres per day`

<Info>
  This formula gives a general guideline. Your actual needs can vary with climate, sweat rate, and diet. If you feel thirsty or notice dark urine, drink more regardless of the calculator's result.
</Info>

## Meal Idea Generator

When you're unsure what to eat, the Meal Idea Generator takes the guesswork out of meal planning. Each time you click **Generate Meal**, the tool randomly selects one item from four nutritional categories and presents them together as a complete, balanced meal.

### The Categories

<Accordion title="Proteins">
  - Grilled chicken breast
  - Baked salmon
  - Greek yogurt bowl
  - Tofu stir-fry
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Carbohydrates">
  - Brown rice
  - Sweet potato
  - Quinoa
  - Whole-grain pasta
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vegetables">
  - Broccoli + carrots
  - Mixed salad greens
  - Roasted peppers + onions
  - Spinach + cucumber
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Healthy Fats">
  - Olive oil drizzle
  - Avocado slices
  - Handful of nuts
  - Chia seeds
</Accordion>

Each generated meal combines one item from every category, so every suggestion is automatically balanced according to the plate method. Click **Generate Meal** as many times as you like until you find a combination that appeals to you.

<Note>
  The Meal Idea Generator provides inspiration, not a strict meal plan. Feel free to swap ingredients, adjust portion sizes, or add seasoning and sauces to suit your taste.
</Note>
